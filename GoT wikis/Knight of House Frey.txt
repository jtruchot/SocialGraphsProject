He has requested the honor of your father's presence on his ninetieth nameday; he intends to take another wife.|The knight to [[Catelyn Stark]]|Cripples, Bastards, and Broken Things A '''knight of House Frey''' was in service to [[House Frey]].  ==Biography== ===[[Game of Thrones: Season 1|''Game of Thrones'': Season 1]]=== He is drinking with companions at the [[Inn at the Crossroads]] when [[Tyrion Lannister|Tyrion]] arrives on his way south. [[Catelyn Stark|Catelyn]], who is also present in the inn, turns to the present knights from the [[Riverlands]] to help her to take Tyrion into custody, accusing him of attempting to murder her son [[Bran Stark|Bran]]. She asserts her origin as a daughter of [[House Tully]], and then tells the knight that she knows his sigil and asks after Lord [[Walder Frey]]. He reports that Lord Frey is about to celebrate his ninetieth name day and has invited Lord [[Hoster Tully]]. Catelyn announces that she will return Tyrion to [[Winterfell]] to await the King's justice. The knight draws his sword and threatens Tyrion, along with the other soldiers loyal to the Tullys."[[Cripples, Bastards, and Broken Things]]" Unlike the other men from the inn, he does not escort Catelyn further."[[The Wolf and the Lion]]"  ===[[Game of Thrones: Season 7|''Game of Thrones'': Season 7]]=== Unless he died sometime during the [[War of the Five Kings]], he is supposedly murdered, along with his other male family members, by [[Arya Stark]], in retaliation of the [[Red Wedding]].  ==Appearance== GoT/Season 1 appearances|4=yes  ==Family tree== House Frey family tree  ==In the books== In the ''[[A Song of Ice and Fire]]'' novels, there is a large group of Frey men-at-arms in the inn, and Catelyn addresses the Captain of the group.
