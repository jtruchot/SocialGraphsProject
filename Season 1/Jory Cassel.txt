= [[Jory and the Stark entourage find the direwolves in the Wolfswood.]] Jory joins Eddard and his retinue as they venture out to execute [[Will]]. He is with the group when they find the [[direwolf]] pups."[[Winter Is Coming]]" Jory later accompanies Eddard south to King's Landing. During the dispute between [[Joffrey Baratheon|Joffrey]] and [[Arya Stark|Arya]], Jory reports that Arya has been found and taken before the king. Jory accompanies Eddard to the [[Inn at the Crossroads|Crossroads Inn]] and listens as [[Robert Baratheon|King Robert]] questions those involved. [[Cersei Lannister|Queen Cersei]] convinces him to sentence Sansa's direwolf [[Lady]] to death despite her not being involved because Arya's wolf [[Nymeria (direwolf)|Nymeria]] cannot be found. Eddard asks Jory to look after Arya and [[Sansa Stark|Sansa]] while he attends to the execution."[[The Kingsroad]]"  Jory assists Eddard's investigation into [[Jon Arryn]]'s final days, unsuccessfully asking for the assistance of Ser [[Hugh]] of the [[Vale of Arryn|Vale]], accompanying Eddard to [[Tobho Mott]]'s smithy and later reminiscing over fighting in the Greyjoy Rebellion with Jaime."[[Lord Snow]]""[[Cripples, Bastards, and Broken Things]]" Jory accompanies Eddard to [[Petyr Baelish]]'s brothel, still seeking illegitimate royal children. Jory is distracted by [[King's Landing whore (Lord Snow)|one of the whores]], while Eddard questions [[Mhaegen]]. On leaving, Eddard has to break Jory's attention away from the woman he is staring at so they can leave. As they walk outside accompanied by two Stark guards, [[Heward]] and [[Wyl (guard)|Wyl]], they are confronted by Jaime. Jaime has heard of his brother [[Tyrion Lannister|Tyrion]]'s arrest and demands his release. Eddard says it was done by his orders and if Jaime kills him Tyrion will die. Jaime has brought many Lannister men-at-arms.  [[Jory's death.]] Jory begins to tell Jaime that if he threatens Eddard again, there will certainly be a fight. However, Jaime interrupts Jory, and he orders his men-at-arms to leave Eddard alive, but to kill his men. They easily spear Heward and Wyl. Jory attacks and kills two of Jaime's men before engaging Jaime himself. As Jaime and Jory trap swords, Jaime stabs Jory through the left eye with a dirk. Eddard, angered and stunned by Jory's death, exchanges blows with Jaime before a Lannister guardsman stabs him through the thigh, ending the melee."[[The Wolf and the Lion]]"  When news about Jory and the others reaches Winterfell, [[Theon Greyjoy|Theon]] insists that [[Robb Stark|Robb]] must make the Lannisters pay for their deaths, but Robb is hesitant about going to war."[[A Golden Crown]]"
